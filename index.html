Global EV Market Share Growth

Global EV sales have increased significantly in the last decade. Globally, market share is at 4.6% in 2020, up from 0.015% in 2020. Numerous factors have contributed to this increase, but most notably are increased EV range with improved battery technology, exciting new vehicles from manufacturers, government tax credits and CO2 regulations , and improved charging infrastructure.

In particular, the Scandanavian countries of Norway, Iceland, and Sweden are leading the way in EV adoption. New models suggest the world needs to mimic this adaption rate to even come close to meeting our goal of "Net Zero Emmissions by 2050".

International Energy Agency(IEA) Scenarios

The IEA 2021 report on status to "Net Zero by 2050" presented two scenarios. The first scenario, Stated Policies (STEPS), projects the world energy model based on the current 2021 policies. The conclusion is that on this course the global temperature will rise around 2.7 deg C and global emissions will fall well short of the "Net Zero" goal.

The second scenario, Sustained Development Scenario (SDS), looks at what will be needed to limit the global temperature rise to around 1.5 deg C and meet "Net Zero" with a likely probability in 2070. A major component of this is EV adoption rate. As you can see when plotting the SDS vs. STEP, the EV adoption rate needs to be much more agressive.
